What is a piRNA

PIWI-interacting RNAs (piRNAs) are single-stranded small non-coding RNAs discovered in germ cells with a role as defenders of genomic integrity in the germline, acting as post-transcriptional repressors of transposable elements. With a regular range around 27 nucleotides, piRNAs are associated with the PIWI clade of Argonaute family proteins (in the mouse MIWI, MILI or MIWI2). Processed piRNAs preferentially accommodate uridine residues at 5’-end and 2'-O-methylated 3'-end to increase stability of these molecules. piRNAs have also been detected in somatic cells with diverse potential functions indicating its high plasticity and pleiotropic activity

Database

We selected those piRBase datasets obtained by immunoprecipitation from wild-type mice. Using a custom bioinformatic pipeline we analyze and calculate each piRNA count per million for every dataset when these data were available. The idea was originaly apply by Larriba et All [1]

In total, 23 datasets were selected (Table 1) resulting in 18,904,992 unique sequences that were detected bound to any PIWI protein. It should be noted that the focus of piRNA studies and sequencing has been on the germline and in particular on the male line. The heterogeneity of the datasets composition assures the presence of piRNAs expressed in different moment of development (adult or distinct embryonic stages), in different biological samples (testes or spermatids) a bounded to different PIWI protein (MIWI, MILI or MIWI2).
